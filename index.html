Frequently asked

Questions, answered.

What is Climer?

Climer is a mobile-first AI literacy app. Each lesson is a 5–15 minute climb that teaches you how to use AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini in real situations — for school, work, or career growth.

Who is Climer for?

Three routes: Base Camp for middle school students, Mid-Mountain for high school students, and Summit Route for working professionals. The same fundamentals at the level that fits you.

Why is AI literacy important right now?

Workers using AI well are collecting a 15–30% wage premium. AI adoption is converging across the US 10× faster than any prior tech wave. The window to get ahead is two to five years — early movers lock in the advantage, the way the early internet adopters did in the 90s.

How long does Climer take?

Each climb is 5–15 minutes. A full unit is roughly 4–14 weeks depending on your route. Designed to fit into a daily commute or coffee break — not a 4-hour video course.

Is Climer free?

Yes. Climer is free during early access at app.climer.ai. Sign in once and your progress follows you across phone and desktop.

How do I learn AI in 2026?

Start by using a real tool — ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini — every day on real tasks. Then learn the fundamentals: how AI predicts text, where it hallucinates, and how to write prompts that get useful answers. Climer teaches both, in 5–15 minute climbs at app.climer.ai.
